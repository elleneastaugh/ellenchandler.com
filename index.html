Toyota/Lexus 2019-2022

Dynamically De-Siloed Communications

The Challenge:

When someone buys a car, every business unit at Toyota has something to say to them. This meant 20+ emails within the first 30 days, putting a damper on the relief and excitement of finally leaving the dealership.

The Insight:

Confusion can quickly turn the excitement of a new car into frustration. But the needs of a used Tundra truck driver aren't the same as those of someone leasing a Lexus LS. Reducing the distance between "new car" and "my car" depended on the car model and trim, the financing, their previous car, the add-ons they paid for, and more.

The Approach:

We needed to match the business units' priorities with those of the individual drivers. I helped design a responsive CRM onboarding experience for new car owners with personalized, data-driven email journeys that halved the number of communications people got in a month and nearly quadrupled engagement.

Philips 2023-2025

Grow The Market

The Challenge:

Lectures from the dentist weren't cutting it. We needed to cut through complacency and convince manual brushers to go electric with a new Sonicare toothbrush.

The Insight:

When life gets better, we take better care of ourselves. We noticed a correlation in the data: people were more likely to change their oral care habit when making other changes in life. And since "bristle sticks" haven't changed much since the dark ages, it was seriously time for an upgrade.

The Approach:

Kate McKinnon's modern toothfairy, named Susan Toothbrush, lovingly negged those going through life transitions into making The Sonicare Switch, driving +9pt ad recall and +2.3pt purchase intent.
